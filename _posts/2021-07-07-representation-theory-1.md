We mainly follow the book 'Linear Representations of Finite Groups' written by Jean-Pierre Serre for the first few(?) posts.

We set $$G$$ as the finite group. For a vector space $V$, let $GL(V)$ be the general linear group (the set of linear map $l:V\rightarrow V$ whose inverse exists). 

A __representation__ of $G$ in $V$ is a homomorphism $\rho:G\rightarrow GL(V)$. For easy notation, write $\rho_s:=\rho(s)$.
